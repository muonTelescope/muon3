# Publication Index

## Locally archived

- `GSU_ICRC2019_Hybrid_Portable_Muon_Neutron_Detector.pdf` - He et al.,
  PoS(ICRC2019)078. Confirms three 20 x 20 x 1 cm scintillator panels with a glued circular
  WLS-fiber groove, a four-channel SiPM readout, 14 cm example panel spacing, coincidence
  plateau above about 54 V for the Hamamatsu generation, and about 120 paired-panel counts/min
  in the reported building configuration.
- `sPHENIX_2018_Design_and_Beam_Test.pdf` - Aidala et al., IEEE TNS 65 (2018),
  arXiv:1704.01461. Documents the sPHENIX calorimeter prototype lineage that motivated the
  GSU mechanical/scintillator work. Its calorimeter response is not a direct photoelectron-yield
  calibration for the GSU loop panel.

## Linked public reference

- Mubashir et al., “Muon Flux Variations Measured by Low-Cost Portable Cosmic Ray Detectors
  and Their Correlation With Space Weather Activity,” JGR Space Physics 128 (2023),
  DOI: https://doi.org/10.1029/2023JA031943

  The public mirror was unavailable during archival. The paper establishes pressure and
  temperature correction as core analysis requirements and compares identical GSU detectors
  in Atlanta and at Mount Wilson.

## Interpretation for the redesign

The publications validate the detector geometry, real-world count-rate scale, and network
science case. They do not provide a transferable, single-photoelectron-calibrated yield for the
current 20 cm loop-fiber panel with MicroFC-30035. The user's existing measured distributions
must therefore set the analog gain, threshold range, saturation target, and cooling benefit.
